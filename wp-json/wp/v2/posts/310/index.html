Although people may see white spots on their teeth as undesirable, they rarely need to be a serious cause for concern from a medical point of view.<\/p>\n

In this article, we look at the reasons why people might get white spots on their teeth, and provide 11 tips for treating and preventing them.<\/p>\n

Causes<\/h3>\n

There are several possible causes of white spots on the teeth.<\/p>\n

A common cause is dental fluorosis.<\/p>\n

People usually get this when they are young if they consumed too much fluoride as a child. It is usually a harmless condition that only tends to develop before the teeth break through the gums.<\/p>\n

Another common cause is enamel hypoplasia.<\/p>\n

This condition occurs when a person’s teeth enamel does not form properly. Like fluorosis, hypoplasia only occurs during childhood when a person’s teeth are still developing. However, it can increase the risk of tooth decay.<\/p>\n

Other causes of white spots on the teeth include poor dental hygiene, especially when someone is wearing braces, or eating too many acidic or sugary foods.<\/p>\n

Treatments<\/h3>\n

There are several possible treatments for white spots on the teeth. The suitability of these treatments may depend on the underlying cause of the white spots and the condition of a person’s teeth.<\/p>\n

1. Enamel microabrasion<\/h4>\n

Some people may be able to have microabrasion done to treat their white spots. During this procedure, a dentist removes a small amount of enamel from the teeth to reduce the appearance of the white spots.<\/p>\n

This professional treatment is typically followed by teeth bleaching, which can make the teeth appear more uniform in color.<\/p>\n

2. Teeth whitening or bleaching<\/h4>\n

Whitening or bleaching teeth can help to reduce the appearance of white spots and other stains. A variety of teeth whitening products, such as strips and paste, are available over-the-counter (OTC.) People can also buy these products online.<\/p>\n

People with white spots can also see a dentist for professional whitening treatments. These treatments tend to use stronger bleaching solutions than those available OTC, which may make them work better.<\/p>\n

3. Dental veneer<\/h4>\n

Dental veneers are thin, protective coverings that attach to the front surface of a person’s teeth. They can conceal white spots and other blemishes very effectively.<\/p>\n

Dental veneers are only available from a dentist and must be professionally fitted. This can make them costly.<\/p>\n

4. Topical fluoride<\/h4>\n

A dentist may apply topical fluoride to the teeth of people with enamel hypoplasia. This may encourage the development of enamel on the teeth and help prevent tooth decay.<\/p>\n

5. Composite resin<\/h4>\n
